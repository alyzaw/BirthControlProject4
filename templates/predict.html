Can our AI guess your contraception?


We created a Machine Learning model utilizing the Tensorflow – Keras module against the Center for Disease Control’s National Survey for Family Growth 2006- 2015 to see if we could predict your contraception based on your demographics. The accuracy of this model was 54% against a test dataset. This model is for predictive purposes only and should not be used as medical advice.

Possible Outcomes

To create our model, we had to bundle some birth control methods into groups. We categorized them as active hormonal birth control (pill, ring, patch, shot), passive implanted devices (IUDs and surgical implants), non-medical contraption (condoms), surgical sterilization, and withrawl/family planning methods. Our dataset showed the most commonly used methods were active hormonal options, passive implanted devices, and surgical sterilization (particularly as users get older), so you are most likely to see these results predicted for you.
