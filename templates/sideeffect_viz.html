How do they stack up?

Well folks, it appears that no matter what birth control method you try, you’re likely to experience changes in your weight and bleeding patterns. These were top mentioned side effects for all methods. It’s possible these changes could mean losing weight or having lighter periods, but given how often these effects show up in negative reviews, we can’t all be so lucky.

The most significant distinction between methods was between IUDs versus all the other options. It’s almost guaranteed that an IUD will cause some pain and discomfort, but both hormonal and non-hormonal IUDs showed significantly fewer complaints about emotional side effects like mood swings, depression, and anxiety. This could be due to the fact that they act locally (in the uterus) with no hormones or an extremely low time-release dose. If you use an active hormonal birth control like the pill, shot, or ring, chances are it will affect your mood. Emotional side effects were some of the most discussed issues for all of these methods.

Overall, reviews for all methods were predominantly negative. This could be due to a bias that users with negative experiences are more likely to share their stories online, but it’s likely because being on any kind of birth control comes with a lot of crummy side effects.

There were no clear winners among the options we looked at, but there were a couple methods that stood out for having the highest rates of very negative reviews: the shot, the progestin-only pill, and the implant scored the lowest in our sentiment analysis. This doesn’t mean it won’t be a good option for you, but you might want to do some more research if you are considering these options.


So what is our final recommendation?

Have a serious discussion with your doctor. Be intentional about your choices and don’t necessarily stick with the first option you try. Pay attention to your body, ask questions, and don’t be afraid to make a change.
